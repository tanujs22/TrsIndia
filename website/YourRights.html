Your Rights!

Delayed, cancelled or overbooked flights could entitle you to compensation of up to €600 per person, no matter the original ticket price. We take care of all interaction with the air company and the tedious paperwork, and we will even bring your case to court of justice if necessary to help you get your money.

Why do I get compensation?

The European Union regulation (EC) 261/2004 states that you as a passenger is entitled to monetary compensation if your flight is delayed, cancelled or overbooked. This regulation also applies to forms of transportation other than flights, such as buses, trains and ferries. Please get in touch with us if you have any questions or a claim regarding this.

On which travel routes can I get compensation?

You are eligible for compensation if you have been:
  • travelling within the EU
  • leaving the EU with any airline
  • arriving into the EU with a carrier registered in the EU, Iceland, Norway or Switzerland

In case of Delay

If your journey is delayed by three or more hours, you (and everyone in your booking) are entitled to a compensation between €250 and €600. This also applies in the case of multi-leg flights operated by the same airline.

You are not entitled to compensation if the airline can prove that the delay was caused by extraordinary circumstances (such as weather, strikes, an emergency or security) that could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken.

In case of Cancellation

If your flight is cancelled you are entitled to a compensation depending on the distance of the flight and the delays experienced before being re-routed.

You are not entitled to compensation if:

  • you were informed of the cancellation at least 14 days in advance
  • you were rerouted within (see table for exact regulations) of your original schedule
  • if the cancellation was caused by extraordinary circumstances (such as weather, strikes, emergencies or security) which could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken.

In case of denied boarding

If you are denied boarding are entitled to compensation depending on the distance of the flight and the delays experienced as a result. The airline is obliged to first seek volunteers to give up their reservation in exchange for certain benefits. In addition, the airline must also offer volunteers the choice between a full refund and re-routing.
